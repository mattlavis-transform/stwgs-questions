Check licences, certificates and other restrictions You must complete the following steps to complete your trade

{% include "includes/results_intro_text.html" %}
  1. Organic goods
    {% if data["organic"] != "no" %}

    C644

    You need to register with a UK-approved organic control body (opens in new tab) to import organic products.

    You must also:

    • get a certificate of inspection (COI), signed or stamped by the exporter’s control body, for each consignment of organic goods
    • notify the Port Health Authority (PHA) or Local Authority (LA) that an organic consignment is arriving and must be checked
    • show your certificate of inspection to the PHA or LA when your goods arrive at the port. They will check and stamp the certificate before your goods go to customs for clearance

    Read the guidance on importing organic food (opens in new tab)

    {% else %}

    Y929

    If your goods are not organic, you may need to show proof.

    Make sure there are no organic references on the packaging.

    {% endif %}
  2. Control on endangered species
    {% if data["endangered"] != "no" %}

    C400

    If your goods are on the CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora) (opens in new tab) list, you need a CITES permit or certificate to move the goods into or out of Great Britain.

    You must make sure that the goods are:

    • accompanied by the CITES permit or certificate
    • imported through a designated air, land or sea port (opens in new tab), where UK Border Force will check and endorse the CITES permit or certificate

    Find out if your goods are CITES listed and how to apply for a CITES permit or certificate to trade endangered species (opens in new tab)

    {% else %}

    Y900

    If the declared goods are not on the CITES list, you do not need a permit or certificate.

    {% endif %}
  3. Control on Invasive Alien Species
    {% if data["alien-invasive"] != "no" %}

    C065

    You must apply for an 'invasive alien species' (IAS) permit from the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA).

    Apply for a permit to import invasive alien species. (opens in new tab)

    {% else %}

    Y942

    You must check if your goods are on the list of 'invasive alien species'. (opens in new tab)

    If they are not on the list, you must have a document identifying the species of your goods.

    If they are on the list, you cannot import these goods unless you're importing them for either:

    • research, ex-situ conservation, or scientific production
    • other activities for reasons of compelling public interest, including social and economic reasons

    You must also have an 'invasive alien species' (IAS) permit from the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA).

    {% endif %}
  4. Phytosanitary control
    {% if data["tree-height"] != "no" %}

    N851

    You need a phytosanitary certificate (PC) (opens in new tab) to import most plants and plant products from non-EU countries into the UK.

    This includes: plants and trees, seeds, cut flowers, fruit, vegetables, wood and wood products.

    Depending on what you are importing, you may also need to:

    • pre-notify the plant health authority (opens in new tab) before your goods arrive
    • pay for plant health inspections

    Read the guidance on importing plants and plant products from non-EU countries (opens in new tab)

    Plant health controls are different if you’re importing from EU countries (opens in new tab)

    {% else %}

    Y501

    For trees under 3 metres tall, a phytosanitary certificate is not needed.

    {% endif %}
